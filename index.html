Intro

Modern language models are typically post-trained via RL to produce a single best answer per query. This matches benchmark evaluation‚Äîwhere exactly one answer is correct‚Äîbut it is fundamentally mismatched to many real-world settings.

In practice, tasks are often multi-valid or epistemically uncertain:

  • ü©∫ Medical diagnosis: multiple plausible (and sometimes simultaneously correct) diagnoses
  • ‚ùì Ambiguous QA: incomplete or underspecified information implies multiple reasonable hypotheses
  • üìö Decision support: downstream actions depend on exploring alternatives and knowing what you don‚Äôt know

Single-answer RL tends to collapse probability mass onto the dominant mode, suppressing alternative correct hypotheses. If we want models to represent distributions of plausible answers‚Äîideally paired with calibrated uncertainty‚Äîwe should optimize those properties during training, not try to recover them at inference time.

Why Single-Answer RL Fails

Standard RLVR uses a single-answer correctness reward: the model is rewarded for matching one ground-truth output. This incentivizes the policy to concentrate mass on a single high-reward response.

As a result, the common ‚Äúfix‚Äù is inference-time scaling‚Äîsampling the model multiple times and hoping diversity emerges. But when the underlying distribution collapses, repeated sampling is fundamentally limited:

  • Duplicate answers (low effective set size)
  • Repeated reasoning scaffolds and redundant computation
  • Poor scaling of Pass@K and recall

Key message: mode collapse is a training objective problem, not an inference trick problem.

Our Idea

Multi-Answer Reinforcement Learning trains a model to generate a set of K distinct candidate answers in a single forward pass.

  • üí° Internalizes aspects of inference-time search into the model‚Äôs generative process
  • üí° Encourages explicit exploration of alternative hypotheses
  • üí° Supports multi-valid tasks and ambiguous single-label tasks
  • üí° Can be extended to produce calibrated confidence per answer

Instead of repeatedly sampling the same collapsed distribution, the model learns to jointly optimize a structured output containing multiple hypotheses‚Äîmore diverse, higher recall, and more token-efficient.

Multi-Answer RLVR (Multi-RLVR)

We generalize RLVR from a single ground-truth answer to a ground-truth answer set \(Y^*(x)=\{y^*_1,\dots,y^*_N\}\), where \(N\) may vary per example.

The policy is prompted to produce a structured set of K distinct candidate answers \(A=\{a_1,\dots,a_K\}\) in a single generation.

We then use a set-level verifiable reward that measures coverage:

$$ R^{multi}_{RLVR}(A, Y^*) = \sum_{i=1}^{K} \mathbb{1}[a_i \in Y^*] $$

This subsumes standard RLVR (K=1) and Pass@K (N=1, K>1), and directly optimizes ‚Äúhow many correct hypotheses did we recover?‚Äù rather than ‚Äúdid we guess the single best one?‚Äù.

Multi-Answer RLCR (Multi-RLCR)

Multi-RLCR extends Multi-RLVR by training the model to output confidence scores per answer. In one chain-of-thought, the model generates:

  • Answer set \(A=\{a_1,\dots,a_K\}\)
  • Confidence set \(Q=\{q_1,\dots,q_K\}\), where each \(q_i \in [0,1]\) estimates correctness of \(a_i\)

We use a set-level calibration objective based on a Multi-Brier score:

$$ R^{multi}_{Brier}(A,Q,Y^*) = \frac{1}{K}\sum_{i=1}^{K} (q_i - \mathbb{1}[a_i \in Y^*])^2 $$

Final reward:

$$ R^{multi}_{RLCR}(A,Q,Y^*) = R^{multi}_{RLVR}(A,Y^*) - R^{multi}_{Brier}(A,Q,Y^*) $$

This trains models to produce uncertainty-aware answer sets‚Äîa distribution over plausible hypotheses with calibrated confidence.

Results

Across medical diagnosis (DDXPlus) and ambiguous QA (HotPotQA-Modified), Multi-Answer RL improves:

  • ‚úÖ Recall / Pass@K (more correct answers recovered per example)
  • ‚úÖ Diversity (distinct hypotheses instead of repeated samples)
  • ‚úÖ Token efficiency (fewer tokens to produce K answers)

Critically, prompting a single-answer-trained model to output multiple answers does not match multi-answer training. The objective matters.

Diversity

Multi-Answer training escapes the dominant mode. Under a matched sampling budget, Multi-RLVR produces substantially more unique answers than RLVR-Single‚Äîrecovering correct hypotheses that single-answer RL never reaches.

Efficiency

Inference-time sampling is expensive because it repeatedly regenerates similar reasoning. Even when the final answers differ, independently sampled outputs often share substantial token-level overlap.

Multi-Answer RL mitigates this by optimizing multiple hypotheses jointly in a single generation‚Äîreducing redundant computation and lowering total token cost for K answers.

Figure 5 (paper page 10). Independent sampling re-instantiates similar reasoning tokens; Multi-Answer RL reduces redundancy.

Takeaway: Multi-answer training is a principled, compute-efficient alternative to inference-time scaling.

Scaling with K

We train Multi-RLVR with \(K \in \{2,3,4,5\}\). Because medical examples often have multiple correct diagnoses, increasing K expands the model‚Äôs capacity to recover valid alternatives.

Training remains stable as K increases: the average number of correct answers per set increases monotonically, and uniqueness constraints ensure gains come from surfacing new hypotheses rather than repeating the dominant one.

Figure 6 (paper page 11). As K increases, Multi-Answer RLVR stably recovers more unique correct diagnoses per set.

Calibration

Multi-RLCR adds a set-level calibration objective so the model can output not just multiple answers, but meaningful confidence estimates for each hypothesis.

On DDXPlus, RLCR-Multi improves set- and answer-level calibration over RLVR-Multi, and calibration curves closely track the identity line.

Figure 3 (paper page 9). RLCR-Multi tracks perfect calibration; RLVR-Multi is systematically overconfident.

Takeaway

If you want models to output multiple plausible hypotheses and calibrated uncertainty, those properties should be optimized during training.

  • ‚úÖ Escapes mode collapse by explicitly training on answer sets
  • ‚úÖ Improves recall/Pass@K and answer diversity
  • ‚úÖ Enables calibrated confidence over hypotheses (Multi-RLCR)
  • ‚úÖ Reduces redundant inference-time computation (token-efficient)

Multi-Answer RL is a principled, compute-efficient alternative to inference-time scaling for tasks where uncertainty and multi-validity matter.
